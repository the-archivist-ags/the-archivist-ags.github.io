That’s all well and good, but public transportation, though limiting emissions, doesn’t solve the overall problem. The real problem we need to deal with is fossil fuels, the main one used being oil. Oil has proved to be a wonderful resource for progress. It helped drive us into the modern age. It powers our cars, heats and cools our houses, and even holds ours food when we have leftovers. But now it’s time to look at its impacts. It’s killing our planet. It’s destroying our home and we aren’t doing enough to stop it. And that’s why I have a plan to crush fossil fuels, starting with oil. We need to ban fracking, which allows for the easier extraction of oil, at the cost of millions of tons of methane being released into the atmosphere, further warming our planet. We need to look towards alternatives for plastic, which, besides being made from oil, also fills our oceans and clutters our streets. We need to look to alternative energy sources, such as wind or solar. We don’t need to defeat cars. We need to defeat oil. People aren’t the problem. They don’t want to destroy their planet. But they don’t have many affordable options. And that’s why we need to aid auto manufacturers. We need to give them some sort of motivation to want to produce cars that run clean. If we can run clean cars, we won’t have to take tens of millions of cars off the road. We don’t have to destroy. We can adapt. And that’s what I will fight for in Congress. I will fight to protect our planet. I will fight to fix the problem with pragmatic solutions. I will fight to create, not to destroy.
