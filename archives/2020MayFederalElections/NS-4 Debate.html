I thank the right honourable gentlemen Hagen for his interests and expertise. About nuclear energy: One of the biggest objections you hear against nuclear energy is that it is unsafe. However, if we look at the figures, it turns out to be the safest form of energy. If we look at the amount of energy generated (kWh) per energy type and the number of deaths this entails worldwide, we see nuclear energy near the bottom of the list. This includes accidents involving nuclear reactors such as those in Chernobyl over 30 years ago. Nuclear energy plants are increasing but incidents over time are decreasing.
The fear we have about radiation from reactors, while understandable, is exaggerated. In addition to the fact that no extra radiation can be measured outside a reactor, we are exposed every day to large quantities of the same radiation that we do not worry about for a second. Such as radiation from the earth, cosmic rays and radiation that we receive from living in large cities. Moreover, there is a minimal chance of any accident causing radiation problems in the area.
The consequences of nuclear disasters are also exaggerated, due to their rareness possibly. The Fukushima disaster has so far recorded one death from the released radiation, while the tsunami (which caused the disaster) killed over 15,000 people. In the Chernobyl accident, many people in the surrounding area (after many years) contracted thyroid cancer. However, this is entirely due to the late and inadequate response of the Russian government, who admitted the accident only three days later. The victims drank milk from the radioactive area days after the disaster, which led to cancer. If the government had intervened immediately and distributed iodine pills, a lot less would have died even in the vicinity of Chernobyl. Therefore, we know that as our society progresses, especially in todays age, nuclear reactors are safer than ever! The choice is therefore easy to make. Modern nuclear energy is the most safe, clean, sustainable and cheap energy source par excellence. France has been running on nuclear energy for three quarters of a decade.
