Drug Enforcement Abolition Act
Summary -
In basic summary, this bill states that, since the Drug Freedom Act has been passed, the purpose of the Drug Enforcement Administration is declared null and void
Full Article -
The Drug Enforcement Abolition Act does 2 things. First of all, it recognizes the Drug Freedom Act of 2018, which lessened regulations, and made it legal to buy and sell substances, in regards to a license. The Drug Freedom Act of 2018, however, directly contradicts the purpose of the Drug Enforcement Administration, a federal-level agency set in place by President Ronald Reagan back in 1973. The Drug Enforcement Abolition Act, the act at hand, simply abolishes and removes this agency from existence. With that being said, yes, it does also ensure that those who work at the Administration are given positions elsewhere in the government, specifically mentioning such agencies as the Federal Bureau of Investigation. If this legislation passes Congress, we can almost assure the public that this will be signed, considering it was written and introduced for the Valerie Administration. Upon signing, it is set to take effect 14 days after the revocation of the public health emergency declared in regards to the COVID-19 virus Written by Dan Reinhard, ANN's Editor-in-Chief —————————————— If you're interested in writing for ANN, please send an article to Dan Reinhard, via email or mail

Congressional Term Limits Amendment
Section I -
Section one of this amendment puts consecutive term limits in place for Senators. These limits suggest that any Senator must wait 3 months after serving 2 consecutive terms to run again.
Section II -
Section two of this amendment puts consecutive term limits in place for Representatives. These limits suggest that any Representative must wait 1 month after serving 6 consecutive terms to run again.
Section III -
Section three of this amendment states that, if someone occupies less than half of their predecessors term, it doesn't count towards their consecutive term totals
Section IV -
Section four of this amendment states that, if someone's term starts before the ratification of this amendment, it doesn't count towards their consecutive term totals. Written by Dan Reinhard
