John Smith

Former President Pro Tempore

The following is a lightly edited transcript of John Smith's interview with The Archivist.

Drunk: Welcome!

John Smith: Thank you for inviting me.

Drunk: Let's begin away from your career. What did you do, if anything, before you got into politics?

John Smith: I was an insurance lawyer, basically when an insurance company tried to screw a customer over, I helped the customer get what they deserved.

Drunk: Where did you start out in politics, and what got you into it?

John Smith: Well I started when I decided to get involved with the Republicans, which were the main right wing party at the time. There I met John Boehner, who has been a guide and friend to me throughout my political career. I started by running for house in a special election. I lost but it gave me name recognition. I got into politics because I had many ideas and wanted to enact them.

Drunk: Could you give us a brief rundown of your political career so far?

John Smith: That's a long story. I lost that special election, I believe it was NS-7. Sometime after that I won election to the Dixie Assembly where served for one term before losing reelection, I believe it was another special election. Then Governor Webb appointed me to represent Dixie in the Senate. I later won a special election for that seat and went on to be President Pro Tempore during most of the Patty administration. Due to a botched primary with the Federalists I didn't go for reelection and instead ran for the DX-2 house seat, which I hold to this day. I later won election to the Assembly to the DX-2 seat which I held until last June. I worked my way to Speaker of the Assembly which I held for quite a while. Sometime after that I started the Conservative party as I was fed up with the Federalists and the corruption that was occurring at the time. This allowed me to become Speaker of the House for a full term and later we rejoined the Federalists after major changes were made. After the collapse of the Federalists I joined the Classical Liberals and have now founded the Tea Party.

Drunk: Interesting. Tell us about the Tea Party. What inspired you to create it, and why did you?

John Smith: Well after the New Republicans failed, they had honestly been dying even when they were the Classical Liberals I saw that there was no political options for the mainstream right wing. So I created a party for just that. Nothing too complicated.

Drunk: Of all the positions you've held, which has been your favorite?

John Smith: While I enjoyed being presiding officer of 3 legislative bodies, they were very stressful positions and I often felt like there was a target on my back. My favorite would have to be as a member of the Dixie Assembly, I feel I've done the most there and enjoyed it.

Drunk: What would you say were the highest and lowest points of your career?

John Smith: The highest would definitely be when I was speaker of the house, it's the highest position I've ever held and I feel the house ran smoothly during my tenure. It's a part I do look back fondly on. The lowest would probably be when I lost reelection to the Assembly the first time, it was my first real defeat and I didn't hold any other positions at the time, not to mention the new administration in Dixie was attempting to undo a lot of the work I had done.

Drunk: What's next in your career?

John Smith: I plan to do some good this term in the House, I'm chairman of the Commerce, Education, and Infrastructure committee and I've already proposed a bill, the Interstate Healthcare Transparency Act.

Drunk: Are there any positions you are eying up for the future?

John Smith: One I've actually been pursuing since the days of Khalil is the Governorship of Dixie. Its been a longtime goal for me and I plan to run for it in September.

Drunk: Interesting. Who would you say was your favorite politician to work with?

John Smith: John Boehner, he's been a longtime friend and we make a great team. We are also extremely similar ideologically

Drunk: Now, as we wrap up, could you sum up your career in a few sentences?

John Smith: I am a conservative dedicated to freedom and justice. I have a very long career and have led political parties. Along with presiding the House, Senate, and the Dixie Assembly. Above all I am a principled man.

Drunk: Thank you for your time, Mr Smith. It's been a pleasure.

John Smith: It has been my pleasure as well.
